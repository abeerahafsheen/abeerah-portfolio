My First Term Exam Experience

In my first term exams, the hardest paper for me was Urdu because I always feel it is a tough subject. Still, I tried my best, and Alhamdulillah, my result in Urdu was not bad at all. I was happy because even though the paper was difficult, I managed to attempt it well. Maths was the subject where I did really great—I got the best marks in Maths, and it made me feel very confident. In English, I also performed well, especially in grammar, where I had prepared a lot. But in SST and Science, my marks were not as good as I expected. Honestly, I thought both papers were very easy when I was attempting them, but when the result came, my marks were surprisingly low. In Islamiyat, my marks were just average, nothing too special. During the exams, I even helped some classmates when they didn’t understand a few things, and I made sure to check my papers three or four times before handing them in. Alhamdulillah, I feel I improved a lot this time, because last year I was not very regular in school, but this year I attended daily and it made a big difference. Finally, when the result was announced, I got third position in my class. My bestie was the topper again—she has been first since class four, and this time also she was a little proud about it. But I was still happy for myself because I know I have improved and worked hard. Next time, InshaAllah, I will try even more and I believe I can be the one to get first position.

The Importance of Books

Books are like good friends. They never leave us and always give us something valuable. A book teaches us lessons, shares knowledge, and even entertains us when we are bored. Reading a book is like having a conversation with a wise person who tells us stories and ideas from different times. Books connect us with history and open doors to the future. Even though we live in a digital age with mobile phones and the internet, the value of books is still the same. No screen can replace the peace and joy that a book gives. If we spend a little time every day with books, our knowledge grows and our mind becomes sharper. Books are not just paper with words; they are a treasure that shapes our lives.

Story One: The Brave Girl Amira

Amira was a cheerful little girl who lived with her family. She had two siblings—her elder sister Ariba and her younger brother, who was in fourth grade. Ariba was responsible and caring, while Amira was known for her fearless and adventurous spirit. Amira loved exploring new places with her friends. She always believed that adventure made life exciting. One day, during a camping trip with her class, something unexpected happened. While walking near the woods, Amira spotted a lion cub trapped in some thorny bushes. The poor cub was hurt and struggling to escape. Everyone else was scared and stepped back, but Amira’s brave heart didn’t let her ignore the little creature. She quickly remembered the first aid kit she had packed in her bag. Carefully, she took it out and, with steady hands, started treating the cub’s wounds. Her gentle care helped the cub feel safe. Soon, the cub stopped crying and began to calm down. Her friends and teachers were amazed at her courage. From that day on, Amira became known as the brave girl who helped a lion cub. The cub, in its own way, also became her secret friend, and Amira’s adventure had just begun.
